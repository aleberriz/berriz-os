## Positioning statement

**"Narratives in numbers."**

A senior data analyst who treats the semantic layer as the narrative layer — the place
where business operators, human analysts, and AI agents all draw from the same source
of truth. The semantic layer is not a BI tool feature; it is a governance and
architecture decision that determines what questions an organization can answer
reliably, and at what speed.

## Five proof points

These are the five things that, together, make the positioning credible and differentiated.
Reference at least two in any bio of medium length or longer.

1. **`intelligence-layer`** — public practitioner knowledge base at the intersection of
   semantic layer design, causal inference, and agentic AI enablement. Not a course log;
   a working reference.
2. **`agent-skills`** — reusable AI skill definitions for version control, analytics
   workflows, and semantic layer design. Aligned with the [agentskills.io](https://agentskills.io)
   open standard; installable as a Claude Code plugin.
3. **Kit** — Senior Data Analyst at a creator-economy SaaS, working on ARR/MRR forecasting,
   marketing attribution, and the data team's semantic layer.
4. **OSI** — active in the [Open Semantic Interchange](https://open-semantic-interchange.org/)
   ecosystem; the frameworks in `intelligence-layer` are designed to be OSI-portable.
5. **MBA + OKR Master** — not a pure IC. Eighteen years of business operations context,
   including OKR implementation and cross-functional stakeholder work.

## What makes this different from a standard technical profile

The combination of:
- Deep numeric/technical craft (SQL, Python, dbt, Omni, causal inference)
- Narrative instinct (The Economist writing and data-storytelling courses; the thesis
  that every metric is a claim that needs a story)
- Business operations experience (MBA, OKR practitioner, 18-year multi-function career)
- FOSS posture and agentic AI fluency (agent-skills, MCP, agentskills.io alignment)

The analogy: a senior analyst who treats the semantic layer the way a copy editor
treats a style guide — not as a constraint, but as the thing that makes everything
else coherent.
